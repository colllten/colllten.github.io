About me

I am a former CS 18000 Head TA and currently a backend software engineer for a large midwest retailer. I have years of CS 18000 experience (dating back to 2020 CS Bridge) and was professionally involved in various capacities from Spring 2022 through Summer 2024. All of my positions involved some form of tutoring and mentoring.

When I started in CS at Purdue, I had no programming background and significantly struggled in understanding the abstract concepts. After a rigorous semester, I ended with an A- in the course and became a Supplemental Instruction Leader, Student Success TA, and Head TA over the course of several semesters.

How Sessions Work

For the Fall 2025 semester, I am offering remote CS 18000 tutoring -- but can be available for in-person sessions upon request.

Tutor sessions will utilize a mix of individual-based activities and the Supplemental Instruction model, which encourages active participation via which allows the student to guide their learning and assert their knowledge in a subject. Whiteboarding, live coding, and mini-program demos will be emplored to accelerate the student's learning.

Upon signing up, I provide a free 15-minute introductory call to break the ice and get to know you better and how you best learn. This is completely optional, but highly recommended as it can only benefit you!

Rates & packages

Tutoring sessions are offered at $30/hour with the option to schedule using 15-minute increments. Sessions can be scheduled any time starting at 5:00 PM and up to 11:00 PM Monday through Thursday. Weekend availability (Friday - Sunday) is available upon request.

Disclaimers

These tutor sessions do not guarantee a minimum grade -- it is up to the student to obtain their grades. LLM-generated code will not be evaluated. I will not do your homework for you, or provide code to solve homework problems.

Tutor sessions are not intended to replace university resources (CS help room, TA labs, Supplemental Instruction, etc.).
